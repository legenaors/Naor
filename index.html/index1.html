Fact 1
It is just a theory!

Let us start from the beginning... The "Big Bang" event is a physical theory ( yes it is actually not a fact )
that describes how the universe expanded from an initial state of high density and temperature.

Scientists' best estimate is that the Big Bang occurred 13.8 billion years ago. But, like so many of the largest-scale properties of the universe, we are not entirely sure about its age.



Fact 2
Space is completely silent

First an explanation of what sound is, sound is a vibration acting like a wave moving through a transmission medium such as a gas, liquid or solid,
That is how we communicate with each other, the sound wave moving through the air.

There is no atmosphere in space or any material, which means that sound has no medium or way to travel to be heard.
So hear me out StarWars fans, yes the Galactic Empire spaceship sound effect is not real ;)



Fact 3
Not that many as you think

Most of us heard already about our galaxy the Milky Way, We heard how vast she is, and contain millions of stars,
But did you know that there are more trees on Earth than stars in the Milky Way?
There are about three trillion trees on planet Earth , and between 100-400 billion stars, approximately, in the galaxy.


Fact 4
Supermassive black holes

You heard it right, if a regular black hole wasn't scary enough then there are giant black holes in the center of most galaxies, And they are a million times heavier than a regular one.
Their enormous gravity field is what shapes the galaxy

We are not certain how they became so big, but we know they started in the early times of the universe from a supernova* like a normal size black hole, they grew bigger the more they "ate".


Fact 5
Not all is gravity

As we learned before the universe was born with a Bang, and began to expand to what we know today.
But when you think about it, gravity is supposed to pull things in instead of pushing them out, Therefore the universe is probably shrinking, right?
No, in 1998 we found out that the expansion of the universe is speeding up.
The reason for this is what we call dark energy/ matter* which has properties of pushing instead of pulling.
If this continues, scientists predict that the universe will tear apart, but that will take billions of years.
